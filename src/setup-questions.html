How Unlocking Works

Memoro Vault uses a two-layer unlock system to balance convenience, encryption, and long-term security.

Layer 1 — Interface Defense:
You select exactly two questions here. These are not encrypted, but they are protected by an exponential lockout timer. No hints are provided. If you know, you know.
This layer is designed to stop casual snooping and ensure the rest of the vault remains hidden behind strong encryption.

Layer 2 — Full Encryption:
All remaining answers, files, and vault data are encrypted using AES-GCM. To access your vault, you must enter every remaining answer exactly.
During vault creation, six answers are secretly selected and their order is randomized using cryptographic entropy. This randomized order is securely embedded in the vault, and not even the creator knows which six are affected.

To decrypt the vault, you must still answer all questions correctly — but six of them must also be entered in the precise hidden order. In a 25-question vault, this introduces 720 possible permutations (6!), and there's no indication which answers are out of order if you fail.

Don’t worry — Memoro Vault handles the permutations for you automatically during recovery. This design makes brute-force attacks (e.g. with tools like Hashcat) exponentially harder, without adding complexity for legitimate users.
